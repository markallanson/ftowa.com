The gloves are off: Panel vs POD

Vehicle:
1996 Mitsubishi FTO GPX

Date:
Feb 2003

Article by:
Mark Allanson - Website


Its a debate as old as time itsself, can the trusty POD filter be relied upon as a simple bolt on mod to generate an increase in horsepower. There have been plenty of articles dedicated to the technical merit of the POD filter over the panel filter. These articles discuss flow characteristics of various filters and produce theoretical results.

Each POD filter manufacturer has their own individual claim to power fame.

"Designed to Increase Horsepower and Acceleration" K&N
"MORE POWER... MORE TORQUE" Simota Air Filters
"...which result in increased throttle response, horsepower, torque and fuel efficiency..." HKS

This article is simple. Very simple, in fact don't anticipate spending more than a couple of minutes browsing some easy to digest information. The ultimate goal of the testing performed was to simply produce two seperate graphs, one mapping before and after power curve, the other to map the before and after torque curves.

With the aim of simplicity, we concentrated on only one brand. All results from these tests are based soely on this brand of POD and Panel filter and should not be taken as a representative of across the board performance of POD filters. The hardware used in this test was a K&N replacement panel filter that sits inside the standard airbox. This is a simple drop in replacement for the standard item. The POD filter is also a K&N item, with a 5" outlet to the intake piping. This means that an adapter has to be used to bolt this onto the standard intake piping. Note that this will hamper air flow marginally. You can purchase larger K&N POD filters with wider mouths, but size starts to become a restriction within the engine bay.
