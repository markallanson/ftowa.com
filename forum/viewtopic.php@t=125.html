as a general rule of thumb when working with MP3, the higher the bitrate in which you encode, the better the sound quality. So if you encoded at 168kbps then you will probably notice the difference, but if you encoded at 320kbps then I could almost guarantee that you wouldn't notice a different in quality.

Also, as a general rule of thumb, when you double the bit rate encoding, you double the file size, so a 3 minute song might compress to 6mb@320kbps, instead of 3mb @ 168kbps.

Still a lot of compression, and a lot more songs than a standard CD. I guess if you want to hear it though you need to get a demo from someone. I think branko had an mp3 head unit, but he has now sold his FTO and bought a '94 prelude vti-r (ugh)

To install subs into cars which also function as a track car you can remove the rear side panels, build a brace to hold you sub to sit flush behind the side panel (just MDF), cut the rear panel (so you can hear the sub), fill behind the inside panel with no more gaps to form a sealed box effect.

Once you done all that just make a grill that you push into place from the back to cover up the speaker.

This way you dont add the extra kilos from the sub box, and if done properly look real good, and sounds just as good as a boxed speaker without the need to drag the box in and out. The speakers (10inch) themselves only weigh about 7 kilos, so if you start dieting now you might be able to compensate for this by the time youve finished.

So dont be scared, grab the jigsaw and start cutting up your car , shell be right mate....
