Read that article from Mark in more detail, and check out all his pictures he included. He did the entire job, and has pics of the whole fix-up job.

He repaired the part in question, but you could probably order a new bit. It's called "BRACKET, M/T GEARSHIFT CABLE". The Mitsubishi part number for the FTO, according to my CAPS program, is MD748651 or MD749667. Which one depends on your chassis number (ie. depends on date of FTO manufacture).

If you pm me your chassis number I can plug it in and tell you which.

Then, ring up Mitsubishi and check price & availability. It's FTO-specific, so they may not have it.

But first, re-read Mark's long, detailed post. Don't jump to conclusions about it being the source of the problem - check it yourself by watching the linkages as someone changes gearstick position. See if the rubber bit has in fact separated in that bracket. If it has, you're that much closer...

Good luck!

- Rich
