Well, overheating isn't really the issue.

The rev limiter stops the engine revving over 8000rpm (actually, a wee bit more), pure and simple.

In a manual - or, in your case, in 'tip' mode - you will simply find yourself bouncing off 8000 until you remember to change gears.

You won't blow up the engine revving it to redline... as long as you run a good maintenance regime. eg. Change the oil+filter regularly (I do mine every 4500-5000km, but I'm an obsessive, hehe). Use the right oil. Keep track of your 3 belts in terms of age, mileage, quality.

There is also an idiot-limiter that stops the engine revving over 6500rpm when cold. But if you're taking it to high rpm in the first 10 to 15 minutes driving from cold, you're simply asking for trouble anyway. Oil takes that long to heat up.

98 octane fuel is also a must if you're going to give it some stick. It was built to run on Jap domestic 100RON fuel... so give it as close as we have here in Oz. BP Ultimate is good stuff.

As for overheating... that depends on when your last coolant flush was, whether you use correct coolant, etc. I've never in 3.5 years had an overheating FTO... although thrashing around for 15 minutes on a track will get your oil temp waaay high!! Just remember to do cool-down time after driving hard - be nice to that machinery and it will be nice to you.

But can you stomp your foot to the floor in 'D' in a tiptronic FTO and be assured of mechanical happiness? Dunno! Any tiptronic owners like to comment? How good is its changing? Does it ever 'forget' to change as the rev limit approaches? Or does it change early, and spoil all the fun?

- Rich
