I was surprised at the difference the slippery diff made to the feel of the launch. Whereas I'd expected it to be easier to hook up, I ended up having difficulty telling if I had one wheel hooked up or two.

With the dodgy old open diff, it was easy. If you were free-revving and not going anywhere, you'd screwed it up and needed to act quickly, back off the throttle and get some traction.

With the LSD though, it was more challenging. One side would be driving me forward, I'd be accelerating, the revs would be rising along with my speed, but I was still (apparently) spinning the other wheel. I'd not really noticed that before. Need lots more time to get the hang of it.

But... seeing as I do sod-all quarter mile runs a year, I guess it's not surprising really!!

- Rich
