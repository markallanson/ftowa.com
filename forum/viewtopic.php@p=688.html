Hi guys,

I have had an experience over the last few weeks and I thought I would explain it all on here. Ill make a workshop article out of it soon.

Over the last 2-3 months my gear shifter had started become a bit spongey when changing into 2nd, 4th and reverse gears. About 2 weeks ago those gears finally gave up the ghost and I have been driving around using only 1st, 3rd and 5th gear.

It was obvious that it wasn't the internals of the gearbox giving up, it just didnt feel like it, so investigation of external geabox components were investigated.

The gear selector input on the FTO is underneath the battery.



Removing the battery mounting braces, you can see the gear selector hardware. This is how it is supposed to look under all circumstances, regardless of what gear you are in.



however, whenever I you try and change into 2nd, 4th or reverse this was happening.



Notice how the outside sheathing was pulling away from the main bracket. The problem here is that the rubber has deteriorated and any pressure causes the sheathing to pull away, hence instead of the cable tightening and changing gears, it is just pulling away the sheathing instead.

This is what the bracket looks like once removed from the car. Notice that it is in 2 seperate parts.



The sheathing / bracket should really be like this at all times, attached to each other with the rubber spacers.



Now, seeings as the rubber spacers are not available over here, and who knows what type of contact adhesive they would use I would most probably have to buy the entire bracket as new from Mitsubishi. Stuff that, far too expensive and I use my car as a daily driver. Time to do a bit of DIY fixing.

Whats needed. 4 small bolts, some rubber spacing and a drill with bit to suit the bolts. Pictures tell a thousand words. You should end up with a fixed bracket, as so.




It is important to note that you should still use some rubber spacers, they are there to reduce vibration into the gearbox. I just used some old conveyor belt rubber I had lying around, but you should be able to buy strips of rubber from a hardware store, and use a stanley knife to cut into the right size.

When installed, everything should look as so.



After you have done this, just reinstall battery mounting brackets and battery and you are ready to go. Gearbox notchyness has improved remarkably since fixing this, so i am guessing the rubber has been deteriorating for quite a while.

Not bad for a Saturday afternoon job, If I had dropped it off a transmission place I would have most likely got a bill for $1000+ for fixing it, so quite a nice saving
