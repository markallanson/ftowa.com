I got the RPW mod done about 2 years ago, the filter got too dirty too quickly mounted in front of the radiator so I re-mounted it back in the engine bay off the standard airbox connector. No real noticable difference to power output, just better sound.. You can pick up a k&n pod from go gear racing services in ossie park for about $115 and make up ur own bracket for free much cheaper and more effective due to less piping from filter to throttle body. Keep the standard airbox cold air intake there for, well cold air intake

Hey Marke,

Do you reckon it's time to post up those torque curve graphs from our "FTO Filter Test" last year?

...before anyone else goes and buys that exact same K&N?


Basically folks, we swapped a particular K&N pod filter on and off two FTOs (Mark's and mine), and did a road dyno sample using each one. The after-market lost power across the entire rev range when compared against the stock induction system. So be careful out there... it may sound faster but may actually be slower!
