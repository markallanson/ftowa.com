1. If your used to the scoobys power find a Manual GPX, that will give you the closest possible power. This is the best model by far n my opinion.

2. If you must go for a TIP go for a 5 speed TIP, meaning you need to look for '97 onwards.

3. There are 2 distinct "categories" of FTO, pre '97 (5sp manual or 4sp Tip, GPX (mivec) or GR (plain v6)) and post '97 "facelift" models (5sp manual or 5sp tip), and GPX (mivec). GR (plain v6) and GPvR (mivec w/bigger bodykit, LSD, stiffer suspension plus a few other things).

the cream of the cropwould be a 5sp Manual GPvR, then go for it, but you will need to look for post '97. They are really hard to find over here. The best alternative is a5sp manual GPX, either post or PRE '97.

Yep, the GP Version R is the top spec one as far as performance goes...

Pretty rare around these parts, but they are around (allegedly).

It's a slightly sharper version of a GPX. Same 2 litre MIVEC (ie. variable valve timing/lift) engine, but with a few other tweaks...

- It's a little lighter.
- Not the full climate control system - no doubt to save weight in stuff like ducts, control units, etc.
- Suspension is a dash lower, and a dash harder (apparently).
- Different rear spoiler.
- Might even have less soundproofing (weight again).

If you want the sharpest possible type of FTO out of the factory, it's worth looking into.

As for the year, I'd get the youngest one I could afford.

As for the handling issue, if you get an FTO with old, worn dampers then it won't feel razor sharp... I replaced mine (and other bits like springs) to get it the way I wanted it. It's been great ever since...

Good luck with the car hunting!

If you hear complaints about FTO's suspension, it is not because it doesnt handle well, but because the suspension with which the car is factory fitted is of very poor quality and wears out quite quickly.

Most FTO's which have done 50k+ will have worn bushes, shocks etc and will need some suspension work done to them. After market suspension bits, although costly, are of much better quality and will last longer and dramasticaly improve handling of a used FTO.

If you are used to a WRX, YOU MUST get a manual, or you will be a little dissapointed.
