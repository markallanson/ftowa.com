I went to an air filter shop the other day and had a chat.
Now they are suppliers of the K&N range of filters but they DO NOT have pod filters in store. Why? because they think it gives u backwards performance.

The reason is engine heat. On the dyno, pod filters seem so great because first of all, the bonnet is up, and secondly there is a BIG fan blowing directly into the engine bay. Thats how the pod gets cool air and performs so well! On the road, all you will get is engine heat resulting in your car going slower.

However, there is a way in making use of the pod. Spending $500+ to SEAL off ALL areas that has heat surrounding the pod. You cannot simply get a tube to feed cold air or block off areas where you think has heat with plastic of foam pieces...

Another thing they also told me is that pod filters were only invented because big performance cars needed more space for their turbos and intercoolers..therefore, took away the airfilter box, and substituted it with the pod. Of course these cars were professionally modified and had money to properly seal off all areas giving heat. This was why everyone wanted a pod. They had seen big performance cars have them..... Pods simply look cool and have a cool sound. Thats the main reason behind people purchasing them... They would know because they sell filters..

I'm no car expert but what they say does sound logical...

That ties in with our testing results. A consistent loss of a few lb/ft torque right across the entire rev range with a pod filter (no reengineered airflow or anything, compared to stock airbox). That was with on-road testing, not stationary.

Mark used to have the "in front of radiator" type pod installation. But it got absolutely shredded by stuff coming in at it, and was caked in dirt. It was uncleanable, so he ditched that setup. That's what prompted a bit of testing of this'n'that.
